# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises. It covers core delivery roles including Developers, Product Managers, Project Managers, Scrum Masters, UX Designers, Data Analysts, and Release Managers.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Scrum ceremonies, remove team blockers, and ensure agile practices are followed consistently across the team.

### Responsibilities
- Guide Sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Coach the team on agile principles and practices
- Remove obstacles and shield the team from interruptions
- Collaborate with the Project Manager on delivery commitments
- Track team health and foster a culture of continuous improvement

### Goals
- Enable the team to operate at sustainable, predictable velocity
- Reduce impediments and unplanned interruptions
- Improve agile maturity and self-organization across the team

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation and impediment tracking
- Sprint ceremony facilitation and retrospective summaries
- Coordination with Project Manager on blockers and commitments

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers advocate for user experience throughout the project lifecycle, ensuring that design solutions meet user needs and business goals.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design assets
- Conduct usability tests and synthesize findings
- Collaborate with Product Manager on feature specifications
- Support Developers with design assets and implementation guidance
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and user-validated designs
- Reduce rework caused by misaligned design assumptions
- Bridge user research insights with product and engineering decisions

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and usability test readouts
- Annotated wireframes and prototypes shared with developers
- Participation in planning and feature spec sessions with Product Manager

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts support evidence-based decision-making by analyzing project and product usage data to provide actionable insights.

### Responsibilities
- Define success metrics in collaboration with Product and Project Managers
- Create and maintain dashboards for key product and delivery signals
- Analyze trends and surface actionable insights for stakeholders
- Work with Developers on instrumentation and data collection needs
- Inform retrospectives with quantitative performance data

### Goals
- Enable data-informed prioritization and planning decisions
- Improve visibility into product usage and project health
- Reduce reliance on anecdotal evidence during planning and retrospectives

### Typical Communication
- Dashboard summaries and trend reports shared with Product and Project Managers
- Data instrumentation requirements raised with Developers
- Metric reviews during planning and retrospective sessions

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee release planning and coordination of deployments to production, ensuring safe and predictable rollouts.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain the release calendar and coordinate release schedules
- Coordinate release steps across engineering, QA, and operations
- Ensure rollout and rollback procedures are documented and followed
- Facilitate stakeholder communications before and after releases
- Manage incidents and post-release monitoring in collaboration with on-call teams

### Goals
- Reduce release risk through consistent processes and checklists
- Maintain a reliable release cadence aligned with business commitments
- Ensure all stakeholders are informed of upcoming and completed releases

### Typical Communication
- Release schedule announcements and pre-release checklists
- Post-release summaries and incident reports
- Coordination with Project Manager, Developers, and QA on release readiness
